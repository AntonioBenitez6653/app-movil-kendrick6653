Kendrick Lamar

    • Biography

    Kendrick Lamar Duckworth was born on June 17, 1987, in Compton, California. His parents, Kenny Duckworth and Paula Oliver, relocated to Compton from Chicago in 1984 to escape the city's gang culture.

    Named after singer-songwriter Eddie Kendricks of The Temptations, Lamar grew up in a working-class African-American family, with his household relying on welfare and living in Section 8 housing, occasionally experiencing homelessness. Despite growing up surrounded by gang affiliates in a city known for high crime rates, Lamar never joined a gang.

    As a child, he was described as quiet and excelled academically. He found an early outlet for expression in writing, which evolved from stories and poetry into songwriting as he processed his experiences witnessing police brutality and gang violence in his neighborhood.

    Lamar began his musical journey as a teenager, adopting the stage name K-Dot and releasing a series of mixtapes, beginning with Youngest Head Nigga in Charge (2003). His raw talent and deeply personal, storytelling lyrics quickly garnered local attention, leading to his signing with the independent record label Top Dawg Entertainment (TDE).

    Under the TDE umbrella, he released more mixtapes, including Training Day (2005) and C4 (2009). In 2009, he co-founded the hip-hop supergroup Black Hippy with fellow TDE artists Jay Rock, Ab-Soul, and ScHoolboy Q. He officially transitioned to using his birth name, Kendrick Lamar, for his first retail release, Overly Dedicated (2010).

    Lamar's official debut studio album, Section.80 (2011), was released independently and was a critical success, establishing him as a rising force in the rap scene. This success paved the way for a joint contract with Dr. Dre's Aftermath Entertainment and Interscope Records.

    His major-label debut, good kid, m.A.A.d city (2012), was hailed as a cinematic concept album detailing the struggles and allure of street life in Compton. It achieved massive critical and commercial success, earning him seven Grammy nominations in 2013.

    He continued to push creative boundaries with his third album, To Pimp a Butterfly (2015), an ambitious work that blended hip-hop with jazz, funk, and soul, exploring themes of race, politics, and depression.

    His fourth album, DAMN. (2017), became his most commercially successful to date, featuring the US number-one single "HUMBLE." In a historic moment, DAMN. earned Lamar the Pulitzer Prize for Music in 2018, making him the first non-classical or jazz recording artist to receive the prestigious award. He also curated and executive produced the soundtrack for the Marvel film Black Panther (2018).

    His fifth studio album, Mr. Morale & the Big Steppers (2022), was his final project under TDE and Aftermath, marking a deeply introspective exploration of personal themes like generational trauma, infidelity, and therapy.

    Kendrick Lamar continues to be highly active. His latest major project is the surprise album GNX which was released in November 2024.

    Furthermore, he has been central to major events, including headlining the Super Bowl LIX Halftime Show in February 2025 in New Orleans, an event where he was joined by SZA. Following this, Lamar and SZA embarked on a joint series of performances for the "Grand National Tour" across North America and internationally in 2025. Today, Kendrick Lamar is widely regarded as one of the most influential and lyrically profound rappers of his generation.
